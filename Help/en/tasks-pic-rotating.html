{[%hh_hdr_en:Common tasks :: Rotating pictures]}

Images taken with a digital still camera are often rotated inappropriately: they are rotated by 90° or even by 180°. To preserve you from twisting your neck and from putting your monitor to a side, PhoA allows you to rotate pictures by multiplies of 90° (i.e. by 90°, 180° or 270°), immediately when you view them.

NB: The program allows for performing a so-called nondestructive rotation of a picture, i. e. a rotation applied "on the fly", immediately before it gets displayed. Due to this the corresponding picture file remains unchanged.

What for this was done in this way? The reason is that most digital still cameras store photos in lossy formats (the most commonly used one is JPEG). A picture compressed in such a format loses quality each time it is saved to a disk file. Resulting difference between the original image and an image saved 2 or 3 times may be noticeable, especially when it is printed.

So nondestructive rotation ensures a maximum quality closest to the original image.

{[%hh_h2:Picture rotation during viewing]}

You can rotate a picture immediately when you view it. In order to perform this click one of the buttons on the Transformations toolbar, or select an appropriate item from the Tools | Transformations menu. You will see the result instantly.

However, the rotation you have applied isn't stored automatically to the picture, so when you view it next time it will be oriented just as before.

If you wish the picture to keep the applied rotation permanently, select the Tools | Transformations | Store transformations menu item. {[%hh_h2:Picture rotation during photo album compilation]}

You also can apply the same rotation to a number of pictures at once directly in the browse mode. Just do the following:

  1. Select all pictures you want to rotate using left mouse button and Shift and Ctrl keys.
  2. Open the Picture properties dialog using the Edit | Edit... menu item.
  3. In the dialog switch to the View & Adjust page.
  4. Choose a rotation using toolbar buttons or with items of the context menu appearing when you right-click the image.
  5. Save the changes by clicking OK.
